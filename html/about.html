About The Reader

My methodology was changed forever when I met my now regular psychic clairvoyant. She encouraged me to dedicate more time to honing what she recognised as a gift. With her guidance, I shifted to intuitive reading, while still honouring traditional iconographic interpretations.

Incorporating the chakra was a natural evolution from studying limbic brain function & its effect on behaviour. A firm believer in the cause & effect relationship between emotion and energy, it’s fascinating to see how they impact the messages given up by the tarot.

About The Tarot

In simple terms, the tarot is a deck of 78 cards, split into two groups. The first group consists of 22 trump cards, known as the Major Arcana. The remaining cards make up the Minor Arcana. It splits into 4 suits, extremely similar to a standard deck of playing cards. First used in 16th century Europe, for a game reminiscent of modern-day bridge.

The traditional meaning of each card comes from the universal use of iconography and archetypes in the Major Arcana. Fortune telling relied on these meanings. Some remain, but most have evolved in line with the world and the sophistication of its inhabitants.

Rich with imagery, they depicted important aspects of the world inhabited by the original players. Their random draw created a unique narrative in each game, influencing player decisions. Part of the game mechanics, it’s easy to see how their connection with fortune telling evolved.

The idea of tarot as fortune telling is misguided. We’d need to relinquish all free will & accept the shuffle & deal of a deck of cards as determining fate & with no other course of action available for it to be true.

An intuitive approach, combining the generally accepted “traditional” meanings with an interpretation of the collective spread & the energy involved, acknowledges we each have free will. It is emboldened by the tarot in considering the past, contemplating the present and offering insights & guidance to create our own future.

About The Chakra

Sanskrit for “wheel of light”, our chakra system starts at the base of the spine & finishes at the crown of the head. These seven energy centres work both in isolation & in synergy to conduct, transmit & receive energy from the earth, universe & our day to day experiences.

Creating a powerful energetic grid of alignment & vitality to power us through each day, our memories, thoughts, attitudes, beliefs & emotions can cause an imbalance in their vibrational frequencies. From upsetting the flow, through to a complete blockage, any chakra out of alignment negatively impacts both our well-being & how we present & are perceived by the world around us.

Meditating on your question while shuffling the tarot is a transference of energy & as an intuitive reader, I often see patterns that signal energetic influences at play. With the same technique, the chakra deck shines a light on the exact element & chakra that requires attention. Weaving this into the context of the tarot narrative layers another dimension of insight & vision for you to acknowledge & receive as you choose.
