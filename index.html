How Does It Work?

First up, how it doesn’t work …

Ask The Tarot doesn’t consider arranging a computer selected range of cards, each with generic meaning attached, to graphically depict a spread on your screen as “a tarot reading”, simply because it’s not.

Plenty of sites do this, requiring you to take each individual, text-book card meaning and then, guided by sparse instructions, piece them together in the hope of producing a coherent narrative.

If that’s your thing, all power to you, but please know the only “reading” that occurs is done by you, following the instructions provided. No computer has the requisite capabilities performing an actual tarot “reading” demands.

A real tarot reading …

Requires an understanding of the tarot and the spreads to use in relation to the question asked, knowledge of individual card meanings, skill in recognising how surrounding cards create nuances to a card’s meaning and experience interpreting holistically (and as is the case with Ask The Tarot – intuitively) to provide a meaningful and considered response. A real tarot reading can only be provided by a human being.

If that’s your definition of a tarot reading you are completely where you need to be.

Selecting a reading …

Attached to each listing on the “Readings” page is a description and other information to help you determine which would best meet your needs. If you have a query still unresolved after perusing each option, please feel free to email it to info@askthetarot.com.au

To request your chosen reading(s), simply hit the associated “Ask Now” button and you’ll be guided through the checkout process. A successful checkout, confirmed both on screen and via email to the address you supplied, adds your request to the queue.

Receiving your reading …

Usually completed within 48 hours of being requested, your reading is emailed shortly thereafter. Depending on your selection, the email will have at least two attachments: an audio file of the reading itself accompanied by an image of the cards dealt in the spread(s).

On occasion, due to the size of the requests queue, your reading may take a little longer to reach you.

If that’s the case when your request is made, you’ll receive an additional email with a revised completion date shortly after successfully completing the checkout process. To date, no reading has taken longer than 72 hours, from request receipt, to deliver.

Querying your reading …

Every effort is made to ensure your reading is both comprehensive in content and clear in meaning. However, should you find yourself unsure as to the meaning or context of any part of your reading you only have to email readings@askthetarot.com.au with a brief description of your query to receive further clarification.

Your email’s subject line should contain only the word “Clarify” and the invoice number of the reading. For example: Clarify ATT21100. Queries should relate only to the recorded content and you must send the email no more than 48 hours from receipt of the reading.

Paypal …

You are free to use your preferred credit or debit card to successfully complete the checkout process. While part of the process occurs on Paypal’s site you do not need a Paypal account, or to log in to their site, to finalise your transaction.

Of course, if you have a Paypal account you can use it in place of a credit/debit card.

Accepted Currencies …

All prices quoted are in Australian dollars, inclusive of 10% GST1. You can elect to pay in USD, GBP or EUR where that presents as an option during the Paypal component of the checkout process2.

As a rough guide3, a reading to the value of AUD $35 translates to approximately:

  • USD $25.50
  • GBP £19.50

For your protection …

You will need to include your address details in order to successfully complete the checkout process. Paypal uses this information as part of their security protocols, designed to protect you.

If you have purchased tokens to Ask The Tarot Yes or No an account is automatically created as part of the initial checkout process. Your account holds your token balance. You will need to log in to your account each time you wish to put a yes or no question to the tarot. Your email address is used as your log in ID.
